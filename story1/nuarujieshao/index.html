Nauru is located in the central Pacific Ocean, about 41 km from the equator to the north, 4160 km from the island of Hawaii to the northeast, 306 km from Kiribati to the east and 4000 km from Sydney, Australia across the Solomon Islands to the southwest. The nearest island is Barnabas (part of Kiribati), 300 km apart. The entire island of Nauru is 6 km long and 4 km wide, with a coastline of about 30 km, a total area of about 21 km2, an exclusive economic zone of 320,000 km2 and a population of about 110,000, including about 10,000 non-Nauruans. Nauru is the smallest country in the South Pacific and the third smallest country in the world, after the Vatican and Monaco. Nauru is an oval-shaped coral island surrounded by a coral reef which is revealed at low tide.
